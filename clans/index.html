How Clan Rolls Work in Paradox

When you create a Paradox character, the game rolls two independent gacha results: your Clan and your Power. Both use the same rarity distribution — 1% Legendary, 10% Rare, 89% Common — and both can be rerolled later with in-game items. Clans give passive stat buffs that persist through your entire progression. Powers determine your ability tree (Shikai for Soul Reapers, Resurrection for Hollows, Schrift for Quincies) and scale with your stats rather than modifying them directly.

Legendary clans are the only ones that give meaningful stat buffs. Common clans — all 34 of them — give zero stats at all, which is why the official Trello explicitly labels them "(No Buffs)". Rare clans fall in between with modest buffs (2-10 points total) that are worth keeping short-term but not worth defending against a Clan Reroll opportunity. If you roll a Common clan, your best move is to immediately redeem the paradoxisout code for 25 Clan Rerolls and keep rolling until you hit Rare or better.

The math on Legendary hunting: at a 1% drop rate, the expected number of rolls for a 50% chance at Legendary is 69. For 63% it's 100, for 95% it's 299. The launch-week code bundle (paradoxisout + Iblamethetesters + storylinefix + anothercodeforfixes) gives roughly 60 Clan Rerolls combined, which is a coin-flip at one Legendary hit. If you want a specific Legendary like Yamamoto or Nkk Le Vaar, plan for 200+ rolls of patient saving — or accept the first Legendary you hit and build around it.

Not every Legendary is equal. Yamamoto (30 total), Nkk Le Vaar (35), and Louisenbairn (29) sit at the top of their races for raw stats, but stat totals do not tell the whole story. Posture is an underrated stat because it scales with your parry window — Halibel's +8 Posture and Haschwalth's +10 Posture shine in ranked and Resurrection PvP where parry consistency outweighs raw damage. Reiatsu-focused clans like Kurosaki, Nkk Le Vaar, and Louisenbairn pair best with Kido-tree skill investment; HP-focused clans like Yhwach and Yamamoto favor Hakuda or Sword builds.

The Common clan list exists as a deliberate design choice: it creates a meaningful delta between lucky and unlucky rolls, which drives code-chase engagement. Do not fall into the trap of "my Common is fine, I'll just play better" — at the top of the progression curve, every 1 point of Reiatsu matters for skill thresholds, and the gap between Legendary and Common is the difference between unlocking Bankai skills at 200 Potential versus waiting until 215.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many clans are in Paradox Roblox?

30+ clans split across three races. Soul Reaper has 22 (5 Legendary, 5 Rare, 12 Common). Hollow has 22 (5 Legendary, 5 Rare, 12 Common). Quincy has 20 (5 Legendary, 5 Rare, 10 Common). Every race has exactly 5 Legendary clans with meaningful buffs.

What is the best clan in Paradox Roblox?

By raw stat total: Nkk Le Vaar (Quincy, 35 points), Yamamoto (Soul Reaper, 30), and Louisenbairn (Hollow, 29). By balance: Aizen, Cifer, and Ishida. By highest HP: Yhwach (+20). Pick based on whether you want raw numbers, flexibility, or survivability — our tier list walks through the synergies.

Can I reroll my clan in Paradox?

Yes. Use a Clan Reroll item from your inventory. Clan Rerolls are granted by most launch-week codes — paradoxisout alone gives 25. Each reroll has a 1% chance to hit Legendary, so plan for 60-100 rolls if you want a specific Legendary.

Do Common clans give any buffs?

No. The official Trello explicitly labels all Common clans "(No Buffs)". If you roll a Common clan and care about stats, reroll immediately.

What happens to my clan if I wipe my character?

Wiping your character with Wipe Essence rerolls your race and progression, but your unused Clan Rerolls in inventory persist. If you had already committed a clan to your race, that clan slot is cleared and you roll fresh on the new race.

Are clan buffs multiplicative or additive?

Additive. Aizen's +10 HP adds 10 to your base HP pool, not 10% of it. Stat buffs stack cleanly with race passives like Quincy's Blut (15% damage reduction), and with the Holy Grail item (+3 skill points).
